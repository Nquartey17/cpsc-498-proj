Courses for majors

This section displays all the courses available under the selected major. Each section of the table will be explained differently:

Name

The name section just has the course name and number. If you want to find out more about the specific course, you can check the Undergraduate catalog or the Schedule of Classes in the Resources section. Not all courses are offered every semester. Be sure to check the Schedule of Classes and talk to you core advisor to see which ones are.

Prerequites/Restrictions

Some courses have requirements that need to be met before they can be taken. This portion of the table displays the specific requirement needed to take the course. If this area of the table is empty, it means there are no prerequites or restrictions to take the course and it can be added into your schedule as long as the course isn't full. If you see this message; "NOTE: The students can select the amount of credits desired for this course ranging from 1-(3 or 6) credits," you should talk to your core advisor about how many credits to take for the course. Finally, it's important to note that most courses that have the letter "L" next to them are labs that must be taken along with the course of the same number. For example CPSC 150 must be taken with CPSC 150L, they cannot be taken during different semesters.

Credits

This part of the table displays the amount of credit hours offered by each course. Note that this is only the credit hours, which is why you may see some courses with zero credits. Some courses do not have credit hours but have lab hours or lecture hours. You can view more about this in CNU Live's Catalog Entries

Pass/Fail

Pass/Failing is an option that allows you to earn the credits of a course without effecting your GPA. What that means is if you choose to pass fail a class, as long as you earn a D- or higher in the course, you will earn the credits for the class and the grade will not hurt your GPA. If you fail the class, you won't earn the credits and the grade won't effect your GPA. It's important to note that to pass/fail a class, you must meet these requirements:

  1. You must have 60 or more overall credits
  2. The course selected cannot be required under your major
  3. GPA must be above 2.00
If "No" is listed under that pass fail option, it means that the course is required under the major you've selected so you cannot pass/fail the class no matter what. If "Possibly" is listed under the Pass/Fail option, it means that there is a chance you could pass/fail the class as long as you've met another requirement. Most courses that have "Possibly" are electives, so if meet the elective requirements, you can pass/fail the class. Also note that the options of "No" and "Possibly" are listed for the specific major you've selected. You can pass/fail any other class is not listed under your major as long as you meet the pass/fail requirements and the course requirements

For example, if you are a computer science major and you have met the three electives requirement by taking CPSC 425, CPSC 470, and CPSC 440, then you can pass/fail an elective that is listed under the major like CPSC 450 if you choose to take it. It is also the reason all of these courses have possibly listed under the pass/fail option for them.

Checklist
